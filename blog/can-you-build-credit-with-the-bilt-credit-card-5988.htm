Can You Build Credit with the Bilt Credit Card?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

In today’s financial landscape, building and maintaining good credit is more important than ever. Whether you’re renting an apartment, applying for a loan, or even securing a job, your credit score plays a crucial role. One card that has gained attention for its unique approach to credit building is the Bilt Credit Card. But can it really help you establish or improve your credit? Let’s dive into the details.

Understanding the Bilt Credit Card

The Bilt Credit Card is a rewards card designed specifically for renters. Unlike traditional credit cards, it allows users to earn points on rent payments without transaction fees—a rare perk in the credit card industry. But beyond rewards, the card also offers tools to help users build credit.

How Does the Bilt Card Work?

The Bilt Card operates like a standard credit card but with a twist:
- Rent Payments: You can pay rent using the card without incurring fees, and these payments are reported to credit bureaus.
- Rewards: Earn points on rent and everyday purchases, which can be redeemed for travel, merchandise, or even rent credits.
- Credit Reporting: Bilt reports your payment history to all three major credit bureaus (Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion), which is essential for building credit.

The Connection Between the Bilt Card and Credit Building

Building credit requires consistent, responsible financial behavior. Here’s how the Bilt Card facilitates that:

1. On-Time Rent Payments Count Toward Credit

Traditionally, rent payments aren’t reported to credit bureaus unless you use a third-party service. The Bilt Card changes this by automatically reporting your rent payments, helping you establish a positive payment history.

2. No Annual Fee Means Easier Access

Many credit-building cards come with high fees, but the Bilt Card has no annual fee, making it accessible to those who are new to credit or rebuilding their scores.

3. Low Barrier to Entry

While approval isn’t guaranteed, the Bilt Card is designed with renters in mind, including those with limited or fair credit. This makes it a viable option for credit-building beginners.

Potential Challenges and Considerations

While the Bilt Card offers clear advantages, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Credit Utilization Matters

Like any credit card, keeping your balance low relative to your credit limit is key. High utilization can hurt your score, even if you pay on time.

Late Payments Can Hurt

Missing payments—whether rent or other charges—will negatively impact your credit. Setting up autopay can help avoid this pitfall.

Not All Landlords Accept Credit Card Payments

If your landlord doesn’t accept credit cards, you’ll need to use Bilt’s check or ACH payment option, which still reports to credit bureaus.

Comparing Bilt to Other Credit-Building Options

How does the Bilt Card stack up against other credit-building tools?

Secured Credit Cards

  • Require a cash deposit as collateral.
  • Often have fewer rewards.
  • May have annual fees.

Credit-Builder Loans

  • Force you to save money while building credit.
  • Don’t offer rewards or flexibility.

Authorized User Status

  • Helps if the primary user has good credit habits.
  • Doesn’t always report to all bureaus.

The Bilt Card stands out by combining credit-building with real-world utility—especially for renters.

Real-World Impact: Who Benefits Most?

Young Professionals and Students

For those new to credit, the Bilt Card provides a way to build credit while paying unavoidable expenses like rent.

Renters with Thin Credit Files

If you lack a diverse credit mix, adding rent payments to your report can strengthen your profile.

Frequent Movers

People who relocate often can benefit from consistent credit reporting, even when changing landlords.

Final Thoughts on Maximizing the Bilt Card

To get the most out of the Bilt Card for credit building:
- Pay on time, every time—set up autopay if needed.
- Keep balances low—aim for under 30% utilization.
- Monitor your credit—use free tools to track progress.

By leveraging the Bilt Card’s unique features, you can turn everyday expenses into opportunities for financial growth. Whether you’re just starting out or looking to improve your score, this card offers a smart path forward.
