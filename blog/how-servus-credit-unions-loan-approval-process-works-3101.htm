How Servus Credit Union’s Loan Approval Process Works

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Navigating the loan approval process can feel overwhelming, especially in today’s fast-paced financial landscape. With rising inflation, fluctuating interest rates, and economic uncertainty, understanding how lenders evaluate applications is more critical than ever. Servus Credit Union, a trusted financial institution in Canada, offers a transparent and member-focused loan approval process. Here’s a deep dive into how it works—and how you can improve your chances of securing the funds you need.

Understanding Servus Credit Union’s Approach to Lending

Servus Credit Union operates with a member-first philosophy, meaning their loan approval process is designed to be fair, flexible, and tailored to individual financial situations. Unlike big banks that rely heavily on automated algorithms, Servus takes a more personalized approach, considering factors beyond just credit scores.

Key Factors in Servus’ Loan Approval Process

  1. Credit History & Score
    While Servus doesn’t solely rely on credit scores, they do review your credit report to assess repayment behavior. A strong credit history (typically 650+) improves approval odds, but they also consider applicants with less-than-perfect credit.

  2. Income & Employment Stability
    In an era of gig economies and remote work, proving steady income can be tricky. Servus evaluates your employment history, income sources, and debt-to-income ratio (DTI). Ideally, your DTI should be below 40%.

  3. Loan Purpose & Amount
    Whether you’re financing a home, car, or consolidating debt, Servus assesses the loan’s purpose. Larger loans (e.g., mortgages) undergo stricter scrutiny than smaller personal loans.

  4. Collateral (for Secured Loans)
    If you’re applying for a secured loan (like a car loan or home equity line), the asset’s value plays a role in approval and terms.

  5. Membership & Relationship with Servus
    Being a long-term member with active accounts (savings, checking) can work in your favor, as Servus values loyalty.

Step-by-Step: The Loan Approval Journey

Step 1: Pre-Qualification (Soft Check)

Before formally applying, you can get pre-qualified online or in-branch. This involves a soft credit pull (which doesn’t affect your score) and a preliminary review of your financial profile.

Step 2: Formal Application

If pre-qualified, you’ll submit a full application with documents like:
- Pay stubs or tax returns (proof of income)
- Bank statements
- Details about existing debts
- Identification (e.g., driver’s license)

Step 3: Underwriting Review

A loan specialist manually reviews your application. They may contact you for additional details or clarification. This human touch sets Servus apart from fully automated lenders.

Step 4: Approval & Terms

If approved, you’ll receive a loan offer with details like:
- Interest rate (fixed or variable)
- Repayment term
- Monthly payment amount

Step 5: Funding

Once you accept the terms, funds are disbursed—often within 1–3 business days for personal loans, longer for mortgages.

How Current Economic Trends Affect Loan Approvals

Inflation & Rising Interest Rates

With the Bank of Canada increasing rates to combat inflation, borrowing costs have risen. Servus may adjust loan terms accordingly, so locking in a fixed rate could be advantageous.

Housing Market Volatility

For mortgages, Servus may require larger down payments or stricter income verification due to fluctuating home prices.

The Gig Economy & Non-Traditional Income

Freelancers and contractors can still qualify but may need to provide 2+ years of income history or additional documentation.

Tips to Improve Your Approval Odds

  • Boost Your Credit Score
    Pay down debts, correct errors on your report, and avoid new credit inquiries before applying.

  • Reduce Your DTI
    Pay off small debts or increase your income to lower your debt-to-income ratio.

  • Consider a Co-Signer
    If your credit is weak, a co-signer with strong credit can help.

  • Apply for the Right Amount
    Request only what you need—higher loan amounts mean stricter requirements.

Servus Credit Union’s loan process balances modern financial realities with personalized service. By understanding how it works—and preparing accordingly—you can secure the funding you need, even in uncertain economic times.
